Statutory Allocation

  • HOME
  • >Lottery Fund
  • > Statutory Allocation

The Lottery Fund is used for public welfare services.

Of lottery sales revenue after prize payouts, operating & administrative costs, 35% of the fund is to be appropriated to legally mandated projects prescribed by law. This amount mostly goes to the organizations that had participated in issuing the lottery before the KLC was established.

In 2011, 10 organizations, including the Ministry of Education & Science Technology, Korea Sports Promotion Foundation, Korea Workers'Compensation & Welfare Service, Small & Medium Business Corporation and 16 local governments were granted their share of lottery proceeds worth 361.2 billion won. These grants are used in consideration of equity and specific needs of the recipient communities for 33 projects, including exapansion of public science education, funding for sports and leisure facilities, and child care subsidies.

Case of Lottery Fund Allocation

Beneficiary : dual-income working class couples
Level of Support : afterschool care for approx.8,330 children per day (1490 community childcare centers)

Support for Low-income Children in Need of Nighttime Protective Care

Low-income famlies are more likely to be dual-income households and less likely to afford nighttime child care services. The lottery fund has provided protection and shelters for low-income children in need of nighttime care through afterschool study program run by Community Chest of Korea since 2006.

This project is being undertaken by 490 community childcare centers and welfare centers nationwide, and nighttime childcare teachers are helping children with their studies after school until 10 pm. These nighttim teachers are hired among job seekers from low-income families in the communities in ways that precedence in employment is given to those with income levels below 170% of minimum living expenses. Currently, 490 nighttime teachers are working to help children in need.

The lottery fund will continue to support low-income households by ensuring that the afterschool study program can contribute to job creation, nighttime childcare, and income guarantee for working families.
