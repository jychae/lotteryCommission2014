Homeownership Program for Low-income Families Living Downtown

"My dream house gives me hope for life!" House of Hope project for the vulnerable

The KLC is doing its utmost to ensure that low-income families have stable housing by supporting housing stabiliy programs for all income groups such as multifamily Housing Rental Program, Public Rental Assistance Program, and Single Room Housing/Hoop House Assistance Program. Many households are receiving stable housing through public and assisted housing Programs backed by the lottery fund.

Housing Stability Program for Residents in Single Room Housing, Hoop Houses

In shanty towns crowded with single room occupany housing and broken-down houses on the outskirt of Seoul, people with disabilities and low-income senior citizens aged 80 or over are struggling to make a living. Now 6,600 households are living in less than a 3.5-square meter single room residence and 5,000 families are staying in hoop houses. In order to provide a place to live to people separated from their families due to economic hardships and those living in substandard housing, the KLC is implementing a housing relocation program for those who want to find a new home.

A single room house for rent at 210,000~240,000 won/month had no toilet, no kitchen facilities, but the relocation program enabled single room occupants to live in an affordable 30㎡ housing unit for rent at about 80,000 won/month with a 1 million won deposit. Families living in hoop houses were able to move in to 1-3 bedroom, 50㎥ houses for rent at 100,000won/month with a 3.5 million won deposit.

With the aid of the lottery fund, 751 housing units were offered to those in need in 2009 alone. In the case of single room occupants moving to rental housing, those able to work are given opportunities to work while those incapable of working get help from religious groups and social service organizations. The lottery fund helps reunite families separated from each other because of financial difficulties and realize their potential.
