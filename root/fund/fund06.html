Art & Culture Programs for the Underserved

As the five workweek system is widespread nowadays and more people are interested in making the most of increased spare time, art and cultural events are drawing more attention than ever before. Unlike urban areas with well-developed human and physical infrastructure, however, residents in rural and industrial areas or with less financial means have limited access to such cultural opportunities.

In accordance with the purpose of the lottery fund, the KLC is supporting programs aimed at promoting art & culture and conserving Cultural heritage to ensure that more people enrich their lives by enjoying art and cultural events. Vulnerable groups, including senior citizens, people with disabilities, low-income famlies, and migrant workers are given opportunities to experience and participate in various cultural events like literary festivals, Korean classical music performances, musicals, dances, and art exhibitions.

The KLC is working hard to tackle cultural inequalities by giving cultural admissions subsidies or funding high quality programs of city museums, galleries, and culture centers nationwide so that people can have more opportunities to enjoy art and cultural events in their vicinity. In 2011, 480 billion won out of the fund will be appropriated to art and culture programs for the underserved.

Case of Art & Culture Programs

Beneficiary : low-income families such as senior citizens, people with disabilities, single-parent families
Level of Support : 0.1 million people/year, less than 50,000won per capital / year

Culture Sharing Program

Minji(4th grader), who comes from a child-headed family, is living with her ailing granmother. Unlike her friends flocking to cram schools after school, she had no fun activities and ended up just killing time. A few days ago, however, she heard good news from her district office that children from child-headed families like Minji will be given opportunities to enjoy movies, performances, or art exhibitions for free.

Low-income families like senior citizens, people with disabilities, or single-parent families (basic livelihood security recipients, medical aid recipient in the second-lowest income bracket) are granted admission subsidies (less than 50,000 won per capita /year) through the culture voucher system fully funded by the lottery money. Besides, free vehicles and food are offered to these low-income groups. In 2009, about 290,000 people benefited from the culture voucher system.

There is also a ticket discount system, called Sarang Ticket, which is aimed at subsidizing admission fees for the low-income class, the culturally underserved, to help them enjoy the right to culture. Sarang members (children and youth aged 3-26, senior citizens aged 65+, residents in small towns) get 7,000 won discount (10 times/year) as an individual, 5,000 won discount (3 times/year) as a group. Nanum members (senior citizens, people with disabilities, North Korean defectors, immigrants, foreign workers) get 100% free tickets.
