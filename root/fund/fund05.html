We give hope and empower our neighbors in need.

People with disabilities, unsupported youth, multicultural families, and our vulnerable neighbors need love and care, seeking our help. In 2011, 290.1 billion won is to be used for people in desperate need of help. From 2011 in particular, the KLC plans to support credit availability programs to improve access to financing for low-income households while expanding projects supporting multicultural and single-parent families.

Case of Welfare Program for the Underprivileged

Beneficiary : married immigrants & their families
Level of Support : 12.9 billion won in 2011

Support for Multicultural Family Centers

Mr. Kim, age 39, is excited about his Vietnamese wife whose Korean proficiency is improving day by day. Now, her Korean language skills are good enough to communicate without any problems in everyday life. Since Mr. Kim and his wife started to talk freely, misunderstandings and conflicts arising from cultural differences between them have disappeared significantly. All this is attributable to the support from multicultural family centers.

Having been told that multicultral family centers teach Korean for free, he visited one in his district, which provides a range of education and counseling services. Knowing that it also offers door-to-door education to those who cannot visit the center in person, Mr. Kim says with big smiles on his face that he will recommend it to other friends who are married to a foreign spouse.

In this way, the lottery fund is supporting multicultural family centers to ensure that married immigrants have access to comprehensive services, including Korean language education, family education, and education on multiculturalism so that they can settle down and lead a stable life in Korean society.
